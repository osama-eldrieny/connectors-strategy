The Problem

Why We Need Criteria Before a Priority List

Without an agreed framework, prioritization becomes opinion-based, political, and inconsistent across teams.

01

Objectivity

Every connector is scored against identical metrics. No connector gets prioritized because it was requested by the loudest voice in the room.

02

Transparency

Managers and stakeholders can see exactly why Connector X ranks above Connector Y — the math is visible and auditable.

03

Defensibility

When engineering resources are constrained, leadership can justify every priority decision to customers, board, or investors.

04

Consistency

Quarterly reviews use the same scoring model, so the backlog stays coherent over time and teams build institutional knowledge.

Weighted Scoring

How the Score is Calculated

A simple weighted formula produces a final score between 1.0 and 5.0 for every connector.

Priority Score Formula
Score = (Impact × 0.33) + (Demand × 0.27) + (Effort × 0.18) + (Alignment × 0.12) + (Ubiquity × 0.10)
Example — Salesforce connector:   (5 × 0.33) + (5 × 0.27) + (3 × 0.18) + (5 × 0.12) + (5 × 0.10) = 1.65 + 1.35 + 0.54 + 0.60 + 0.50 = 4.64 → CRITICAL
Priority Bands

The 4 Priority Tiers

Once scored, every connector falls into one of four tiers that inform resource allocation and roadmap planning.

CRITICAL
4.0+
Build Next
High impact + market demand. These are the connectors that unlock revenue, block customer wins, or fill competitive gaps. Prioritize immediately.
HIGH
3.0–3.9
Build in Current Quarter
Solid strategic value. Schedule them in the current or next planning cycle. Worth the engineering effort.
MEDIUM
2.0–2.9
Build as Capacity Allows
Nice-to-have. Include in the backlog and plan them only when higher tiers are satisfied or if a customer escalates.
LOW
< 2.0
Research / Track
Low strategic value. Monitor for emerging demand, but don't allocate engineering time yet.
