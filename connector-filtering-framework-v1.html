Establish Your Filtering Criteria

Before evaluating individual connectors, your team needs to define the strategic context. Answer these questions once — they become the framework for filtering down from 12,000 connectors to 200 that matter most.

1

What is our product roadmap for the next 12–18 months?

Identify strategic priorities: which verticals, use cases, and workflows are we building? This defines which connectors align with where we're headed.

2

Who is our ideal customer profile (ICP)?

Define company size, industry, and revenue threshold. Understanding who we serve tells us which connectors they depend on.

3

What connectors do our customers request + what do competitors support?

Combine two signals: customer demand (support tickets, deal-blockers, feedback) and competitive necessity (what all competitors offer). This is market reality.

4

How many connectors can we build and maintain?

Resource constraint: what is our engineering capacity this year? How many integrations can our support team handle? This sets the ceiling.

5

What is the API quality distribution? (Easy/Medium/Hard to build)

Understand the build complexity landscape. Which connectors have good APIs? Are there security/compliance blockers? This informs feasibility.
